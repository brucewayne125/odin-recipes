Doro Wot

Doro wot is a spicy, slow-cooked chicken stew, considered the national dish of Ethiopia and Eritrea. The name translates simply to "chicken stew," but the preparation is a labor-intensive process that results in a complex, rich, and flavorful dish.

Ingredients

  • Onions = 2 kilos
  • Garlic = 2 Cloves
  • Ginger powder = 1/2 tablespoon
  • Cumen = 1/2 tablespoon
  • Paper = 1 Tablespoon
  • Salt = 1 1/2 Tablespoon
  • Ethiopian Butter = 1/2 kilo
  • Berbere = 1/2 kilo
  • Eggs = 3

Steps

  1. Finely chop the onions and cook them with the garlic until brown with cooking oil
  2. Add the berbere and mix thorouhly with the onion and let it cook for 20 minutes
  3. Add salt
  4. Add the chicken and mix it well with the stew. Cook it for 10 minutes
  5. Add 2 litres of water
  6. Add Butter
  7. Add pepper
  8. Add the Ginger and Cumun
  9. Cook for 20-30 minutes
  10. Add the Boiled Eggs
